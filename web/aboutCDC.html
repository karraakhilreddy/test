The College Development Council (CDC) shall provide help and guidance for the integrated development of affiliated colleges in the University. The Council shall provide co-ordination between the University/State Government/TSCHE/AICTE/NCTE/PCI/BCI etc. and the Colleges. The College Development Council accords permission/affiliation to establish and maintain a college, subject to fulfillment of stipulated conditions of the University/State Government/TSCHE/AICTE/NCTE/PCI/BCI. It also helps in the all-round development of the College, subject to the commitment of the Management in abiding to the rules and regulations of different Academic Bodies, State Government and University from time to time.

FUNCTIONS OF THE COLLEGE DEVELOPMENT COUNCIL

  • The Telangana State Council of Higher Education (TSCHE) accords permission to colleges to start Under-Graduate or Post-Graduate Courses. The CDC shall co-ordinate the joint inspections for starting of new colleges or courses.
  • The All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) accords permission for B.Tech., M.Tech., MBA and MCA, Colleges.
  • The Pharmacy Council of India (PCI) and The All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) accords permission for B.Pharmacy, M.Pharmacy, Pharm.D. and Pharm.(PB) Colleges.
  • National Council for Technical Education (NCTE) accords permission for Education and Physical Education Colleges.
  • BAR COUNCIL OF INDIA (BCI) for Law Colleges.
  • Based on the permission of different Academic Bodies and after inspection of Joint Inspection Committee, University Affiliation Committee along with Dean, CDC verifies the infrastructural facilities, laboratories, faculty and other amenities. After submission of the Inspection Report and its approval by the Vice-Chancellor, the CDC accords affiliation to the colleges to establish and maintain the courses.
  • Every year, CDC conducts Affiliation-Inspections to the University Affiliated Colleges for extending the temporary affiliation from time to time.
  • CDC also co-ordinates between the Colleges (which are included under section 2 (f) and 12 (B) of UGC Act) for development grants and their proper utilization.

PROCEDURE FOR ESTABLISHMENT OF A NEW DEGREE COLLEGE

  1. The Telangana State Council of Higher Education (TSCHE) shall issue Notification during November/December in every year.
  2. The interested Societies shall submit proposals in the prescribed format to the TSCHE.
  3. The TSCHE shall arrange counseling for rectification of anomalies, if any.
  4. The TSCHE, in co-ordination with the University, shall arrange Joint Inspections to the successful applicants.
  5. The University shall recommend/forward the Joint Inspection Reports (JIC) Reports through the College Development Council with Vice-Chancellorâ€™s approval.
  6. The TSCHE shall issue proceedings sanctioning the college as per the reports of JIC and University.
  7. The University, through the College Development Council shall arrange inspections for granting affiliation.
  8. Affiliation orders shall be issued by the University (June/July).
